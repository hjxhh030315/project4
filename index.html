Stress Signals: How Physiology Reflects Academic Pressure

Explore how wearable data reveals student stress during exams

By Jessica Jiaxin, Neil, Alex, Seichii

Project Overview

This project visualizes real-world stress responses using wearable physiological data collected during midterms and finals. By analyzing heart rate, electrodermal activity, and other signals, we uncover how cognitive stress affects students under pressure.

What We Learned

  • Heart rate tends to spike just before the end of each exam.
  • Female students showed greater variability in EDA during finals.
  • Stress patterns differ more by individual than by exam type.

Dataset & Methodology

We used the Wearable Exam Stress Dataset from PhysioNet. Signals were aggregated and cleaned using Python, then visualized with D3.js. Preprocessing involved resampling, timestamp conversion, and grouping by student and exam session.
